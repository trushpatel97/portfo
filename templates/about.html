03 : About me

Highschool

In highschool, I had Electrical Engineering expierence. I built a lot of projects such as a stereo speaker and continuity tester from scratch. Once I got to advanced electronics in junior year, I programmed an Arduino servo to continually mix an etching solution used for PCBs. That same year, I was selected by the teacher to lead a group of beginner level students to make a RC Car that uses a GPS to track the car. Click here to read about it!

College

From 2016-2018 I attended community college at Montgomery County Community College. I transferred out with a 3.41 GPA and received a Dean's list award. Throughout my time there, I made three huge projects: Color Proportions, Cruelty-Free Mousetrap, and a Vet Database.
In Fall 2018, I started my first semester at Temple University. The classes I have taken at Temple so far, have not required us to create our own project. Therefore, I started making projects from home on my free time.

Work Experience

During my Fall 2018 semester, through October 2018 to December 2018, I worked part-time seasonal job at Cherrydale. It is a company that packages and manufactures fundraiser items for schools. I trained new employees quite a bit, especially those who couldn't understand English. However, when I wasn't training, I was in charge of packaging and scanning items.
